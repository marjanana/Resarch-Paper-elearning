Annotated Bibliography

Ezra, Kate. Art of the Dogon Selections from the Lester Wunderman Collection. New York: The Metropolitan Museum of Art, 1988.

The book analyzes various Dogon artworks and provides historical context for understanding their art Information about Seated Couple is on page 65. The author the symmetry and explains how it reflects the relationship between man and woman. The notions of “equation between male and female” and “sexual duality” are discussed. The book also lists iconographic elements, such as a labret, quiver, and a child figure in the back; it also gives information on how the size of the sculpture relates to its use.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art. “Figure: Seated Couple (18th-early 19th Century).” Accessed April 21, 2019. http://www.metmuseum.org/toah/works-of-art/1977.394.15/

This article provides basic information about the piece. It discusses how the ideal relationship between man and woman in Dogon society is shown through the similarity in form of each figure. The article mentions “bilateral symmetry” of form and the elongation of the figures’ bodies. It also explains how the sculpture shows the equal importance of man and women in a family. The intended purpose of the sculpture is given as well.

LaGama, Alisa. Echoing Images: Couples in African Sculpture. New Haven and London: Yale University Press, 2004.

The author discusses human relationships, the concepts of duality, sexuality, masculinity, femininity, etc.

Van Beek, Walter E. A. "Functions of Sculpture in Dogon Religion." African Arts 21, no. 4 (August 1988): 58-91.

On page 63, the author interprets the posture of the figures and discusses the iconography of the stool. The source does not discuss Seated Couple, but rather presents the taxonomy of the Dogon sculpture.
